# DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES: Wrong Side of Right

## Chapter 1: Wrong Side of Everything

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Jake Cooper, first person  
**Setting:** Jake's rental house, streets of Millbrook, Sam's Garage  
**Time:** Early morning, first week of summer 1957

**Purpose:** Establish Jake's world, reputation, and daily life on the wrong side of the tracks.

**Beats:**
- Jake wakes in small rental house, mother already gone to diner shift, surveys his sparse room with motorcycle parts and library book hidden under bed
- Rides motorcycle across railroad tracks from east side to Sam's Garage, noting the dividing line between respectable Millbrook and his world
- Arrives at garage, Sam assigns transmission job, Jake finds peace working with engines where skill matters more than last name
- Tommy stops by during lunch, they joke about drag racing tonight, discuss summer plans and limited options
- Sam mentions Mayor Hayes's Buick being towed in with engine trouble, Jake shrugs it off as just another repair job

**Dialogue Topics:** Tommy teasing about summer being boring, Sam warning Jake to stay clean, Jake's short responses showing he's accepted his lot, casual banter masking Jake's awareness of his reputation.

**Emotional Arc:** Jake starts resigned to his place in town's hierarchy, finding brief moments of competence and peace in engine work, but underlying current of anger and acceptance that this is all his future holds.

**Key Details:** 1957 summer heat, motorcycle Jake rebuilt himself, railroad tracks as literal town divider, grease permanently under Jake's fingernails, Sam's Korean War limp, leather jacket despite heat.

## Chapter 2: She Walks In

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Jake Cooper, first person  
**Setting:** Sam's Garage  
**Time:** Mid-afternoon, same day

**Purpose:** Elizabeth enters Jake's world for the first time, creating immediate contrast and attraction.

**Beats:**
- Jake's working under a car when he hears unfamiliar footsteps, not work boots but lighter shoes, stands and sees Elizabeth Hayes in garage doorway
- Elizabeth explains father's Buick broke down, asks when it will be ready, looks out of place but doesn't show disgust at garage environment
- Jake keeps responses short and professional, won't meet her eyes, hyper-aware of her neat dress and pearls versus his grease-stained appearance
- Elizabeth asks Jake his name, thanks him specifically, watches him work for moment before Sam quotes repair timeline
- After she leaves, Jake tries to shake off the encounter but catches himself thinking about how she looked at his hands on the engine

**Dialogue Topics:** Elizabeth's polite questions about repair timeline, Jake's terse technical answers, Sam's formal price quote, Elizabeth thanking Jake by name after asking for it.

**Emotional Arc:** Jake starts defensive and closed off, determined to be invisible to someone from her world, but despite himself notices details about her and feels unsettled by the encounter.

**Key Details:** Elizabeth's white gloves and pearls, her curious rather than disgusted expression, the 1955 Buick's pristine condition, Jake's awareness of grease under his nails, her use of his name.

## Chapter 3: Just a Car

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Jake Cooper, first person  
**Setting:** Sam's Garage  
**Time:** Two days later, afternoon

**Purpose:** Show Jake's skill and care in his work, Elizabeth's return reveals mutual interest.

**Emotional Arc:** Jake starts determined to treat this as just another job, but finds himself taking pride in showing his work and skill, ends unsettled by how much he talked and how good it felt to have someone genuinely interested.

**Key Details:** Fuel pump parts laid out on workbench, Jake's grease-stained hands demonstrating components, Elizabeth leaning in to see better, her perfume mixing with motor oil smell, Jake's careful handwriting on the invoice.

## Chapter 6: Teaching Her

**Setting:** Sam's Garage

**Purpose:** First real time together, Elizabeth proves herself, walls start coming down.

**Emotional Arc:** Jake starts expecting to scare her off with actual work, grows impressed by her determination and lack of squeamishness, ends looking forward to next time despite knowing he shouldn't.

**Key Details:** Elizabeth's practical work clothes, grease under her fingernails she doesn't wipe away, her genuine pride in using lug wrench correctly, notebook where she writes down his instructions, sun through garage windows.

## Chapter 9: The Line

**POV:** Jake Cooper, first person

**Dialogue Topics:** Jake's attempts to list reasons they should stop, Elizabeth's counterarguments about authenticity versus approval, Jake admitting fear of not being enough, Elizabeth saying she wants him not someone safe, honesty about attraction and impossibility.

**Emotional Arc:** Jake starts trying to be rational and protective, Elizabeth's honesty and courage break through his defenses, kiss changes everything, ends both frightened and exhilarated by what they've started.

**Key Details:** Quarry at twilight becoming their private place, Jake's hands shaking when he almost touches her face, Elizabeth's directness in saying what she wants, softness of first kiss, them pulling apart breathing hard, stars starting to appear overhead.
